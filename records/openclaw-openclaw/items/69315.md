## Summary

Keep open. Current main still awaits TTS synthesis inside final, tool, and block reply delivery before routing the payload, and Telegram ordinary messages are intentionally serialized per chat/topic, so slow TTS can still hold later same-topic inbound work. The related #42539 covers a narrower text-plus-voice delivery-mode request, but it does not supersede this broader non-blocking/background TTS request.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this issue open for maintainer design. The likely direction is a shared TTS delivery policy under `messages.tts` plus a background delivery path that sends visible text immediately, generates audio with bounded concurrency, and posts or attaches the resulting voice/audio through the existing channel delivery abstractions without blocking the active inbound turn. Coordinate with #42539 so Telegram text-plus-voice behavior and async delivery do not diverge.

## Risks / Open Questions

- A fix needs an explicit product choice between text-first background audio, bounded parallel TTS, or a configurable compatibility mode; each has different transcript, ordering, and per-channel UX implications.

- Telegram delivery needs careful treatment of same-topic ordering, voice-note privacy errors, captions, and whether audio is sent as an edit/attachment or follow-up voice note.
