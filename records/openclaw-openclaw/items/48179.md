## Summary

Keep open. Current main has Telegram voice-note transcription and voice-note TTS delivery, but it still lacks the requested voice-specific priority lane, streaming STT/TTS pipeline, concurrent voice/text processing model, result/preset caching, and stage-boundary timing snapshots. Related PR #39075 is adjacent Telegram pipeline work, but it is open and narrower than this issue.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this open as the Telegram voice pipeline performance tracking item. A strong implementation should add stage timing/state snapshots first, benchmark current STT/TTS latency, then design a voice-aware priority/concurrency policy that preserves Telegram chat/topic ordering. Streaming and caching should be added through shared media-understanding and speech-provider contracts where providers support them, with #39075 treated as adjacent prior art rather than a shipped fix.

## Risks / Open Questions

- A voice bypass or concurrent lane could reorder per-chat/per-topic Telegram turns, approvals, aborts, BTW side questions, preview edits, or final delivery unless the sequencing contract is designed explicitly.
- Streaming STT/TTS support varies by provider; exposing it safely likely needs additive media-understanding and speech-provider contracts rather than a Telegram-only shortcut.
- Caching transcription results or TTS outputs needs privacy, invalidation, and per-chat/account/provider scoping so repeated voice notes do not leak or reuse sensitive audio across sessions.
- The requested latency targets need benchmarks and a provider matrix; source inspection cannot prove sub-3s STT or sub-2s TTS for configured providers.
