## Summary

Keep this issue open. Current main has a narrow safeguard that suppresses a mutating-tool warning only when the assistant explicitly acknowledges the failed action, but it still intentionally emits a user-facing `⚠️ ... failed` payload for failed `edit`/write-style tools when the assistant claims success or does not acknowledge the failure. Telegram still delivers final `isError` payloads through the normal reply path. The merged #72410 appears to cover part of the problem, while open #73750 and broader #39406 show the remaining policy is still under active maintainer handling rather than ready for cleanup closure.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep this open until maintainers settle the user-facing policy for failed mutating tools. The best path is to review #73750 or replace it with a focused fix that prevents raw failed-edit/tool warning text from leaking into Telegram, keeps full details in logs/debug surfaces, preserves a clean fallback when the failure is unresolved or the assistant claims success, and leaves #39406 open for the broader configurable delivery-policy request.
