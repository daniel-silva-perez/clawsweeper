## Summary

Keep this issue open. Current main still keeps followup queue items in process memory, still rejects drain-window command enqueues with `GatewayDrainingError`, and does not add a durable pending inbound/followup replay path; the open PRs #51623 and #51803 are still the appropriate implementation candidates to review rather than closing this early.

## What This Changes

Request to persist and replay queued or in-flight inbound work across gateway restarts, covering followup queues, drain-window arrivals, and provider downtime catch-up.

## Best Possible Solution

Keep the issue open until maintainers review and either land or close one of the phase-1 followup persistence PRs, then explicitly decide whether drain-window arrivals and provider downtime catch-up remain here or move to narrower trackers such as #55792 and #74424.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes. The code-level reproduction is high confidence: queue a followup in `FOLLOWUP_QUEUES`, restart before drain completion, and the process-local map is lost; during restart drain, `markGatewayDraining()` makes new command enqueues reject with `GatewayDrainingError`.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear for the full three-phase request. Durable followup persistence is the right first step, but the safest final solution should use a typed durable handoff with dedupe/TTL and channel-specific ACK or catch-up hooks where provider contracts support them.
