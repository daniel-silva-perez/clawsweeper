## Summary

Keep open. Current `main` has meaningful narrow mitigations for Bonjour churn and bundled runtime-dependency repair, but it still preserves the broader default-on bundled-plugin startup policy that the reporter, maintainer comment, and later data points identify as the remaining upgrade/startup problem.

## What This Changes

The issue requests that upgrades avoid enabling or initializing a broad set of unused bundled plugins in a way that can block gateway startup, with Bonjour and bundled runtime-dependency staging called out as concrete failure surfaces.

## Best Possible Solution

Settle the product policy for gateway startup after upgrades: either make optional bundled startup services such as Bonjour configured-only/opt-in, or introduce a supported minimal/safe profile that loads only configured operational plugins while status, health, runtime-deps repair, and provider recovery report optional bundled-plugin failures without blocking readiness.

## Reproduction Assessment

Yes for the remaining policy shape: current `main` statically reproduces Bonjour as default-on and default-startup, plus default-enabled non-provider bundled runtime-deps inclusion under empty config. The original Debian resource-constrained restart churn has useful logs but was not live-reproduced here.

## Solution Assessment

Unclear until maintainers choose the startup policy. The Bonjour restart cap and discovery timeout are good narrow mitigations, but they are not the complete fix for the upgrade/default bundled-plugin surface described by the reporter and later comments.
