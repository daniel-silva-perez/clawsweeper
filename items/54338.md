## Summary

Current `main` shows `cron` is intentionally owner-only and is filtered before sandbox tool-policy checks. The reported DM-vs-group difference matches sender ownership resolution, not sandbox injection, and `sandbox explain` is documented to report sandbox policy only.

## Risks / Open Questions

- This review was read-only and did not run a live reproduction against the reporter's custom `wechat-ipad` channel plugin.
- The DM-vs-group difference is most likely explained by plugin-specific owner resolution (`senderIsOwner`) rather than sandbox policy, but that specific custom plugin is not in this repository.

## Close Comment

Closing after Codex review of current `main` (`8f64cd3e4d8339a6aab3ca230d5e14944c91cfaf`). I could not confirm a sandbox-specific `cron` injection bug in OpenClaw core. On current `main`, `cron` is intentionally an owner-only tool (`src/agents/tools/owner-only-tools.ts:1`, `src/agents/tools/cron-tool.ts:419`), and owner-only filtering happens before the sandbox tool-policy pass (`src/agents/pi-tools.ts:671`). Current tests also assert that non-owner senders do not get `cron` while owner senders do (`src/agents/pi-tools.owner-only-gating.test.ts:23`). Separately, `openclaw sandbox explain` is documented to show sandbox mode/tool policy/elevated gates, not owner authorization (`docs/cli/sandbox.md:30`). Given the report's custom `wechat-ipad` channel plugin and the DM-vs-group split, this looks like sender owner-resolution on that channel surface rather than sandbox policy in OpenClaw core. If this still reproduces on a bundled channel with explicit `ownerAllowFrom`/owner identity configured, please open a fresh issue with the exact channel, owner config, and `senderIsOwner`/session context details.
