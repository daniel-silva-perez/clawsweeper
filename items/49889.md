## Summary

Current `main` has partial mitigation for Telegram partial-stream finalization observability, but it does not fully implement the issue’s requested delivery-mode visibility or hook parity. `preview-finalized` now emits an internal `message:sent` hook, and preview-edit failures log more specific fallback/retain reasons, but other final outcomes remain non-uniformly observable and plugin-hook parity is still missing.

## Evidence

- **Preview-finalized only bridges the internal hook:** `extensions/telegram/src/bot-message-dispatch.ts:647` defines `emitPreviewFinalizedHook`, and `extensions/telegram/src/bot-message-dispatch.ts:824` only calls it for `result.kind === "preview-finalized"`. This does not emit an explicit outcome for `preview-retained`, `sent`, or `skipped` from the lane-delivery path.
  - file: [extensions/telegram/src/bot-message-dispatch.ts:647](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/45146913007da723b4f06aba6a66cf4a695974c0/extensions/telegram/src/bot-message-dispatch.ts#L647)
  - command: `nl -ba extensions/telegram/src/bot-message-dispatch.ts | sed -n '640,840p'`
  - sha: [45146913007d](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/commit/45146913007da723b4f06aba6a66cf4a695974c0)
- **Outcome model still omits several requested states:** `extensions/telegram/src/lane-delivery-text-deliverer.ts:59` models only `preview-finalized`, `preview-retained`, `preview-updated`, `sent`, and `skipped`. The issue explicitly asked for easy observability of `hook-canceled` and `transport-failed`, but those are not represented as first-class lane-delivery outcomes.
  - file: [extensions/telegram/src/lane-delivery-text-deliverer.ts:59](https://github.com/openclaw/openclaw/blob/45146913007da723b4f06aba6a66cf4a695974c0/extensions/telegram/src/lane-delivery-text-deliverer.ts#L59)

That leaves one of the issue’s requested observability buckets unresolved.
